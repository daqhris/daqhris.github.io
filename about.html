about me


Chris A. IRADUKUNDA was born in 1992 in Bujumbura, Burundi. He attended primary school at École Primaire Stella Matutina and secondary school at Lycée du Saint-Esprit de Bujumbura with an emphasis on Maths and Physics. In 2012, he enrolled to study Computer Science and Engineering (taught in Chinese) at University of Science and Technology, Beijing (China). As of 2017, he is pursuing his passion in the same field at University of Namur (Belgium) and he volunteers to work at a bicycle repair shop.

Besides from being both a fast-learner and a hard-working individual, Mr. IRADUKUNDA strives to lend a hand to his local community and brings to fruition diverse side projects.

During primary school, he was involved in the Scout Movement as part of the Charles de Foucauld unit. Then, at the Jesuit's high school, he participated in various on-campus student clubs which ultimately resulted in leading the Akanuma Journal as editor-in-chief. At graduation, he received the Best Devotion to School Award and a short while after, he was granted a full-ride scholarship at a Chinese university.

Over a five-year period, while he was living in Beijing, he toured the northeastern and the northwestern regions of China and gladly experienced its fabulous culture and history. Concurrently, he worked part-time as a background actor and as a foreign talent manager for major Chinese blockbusters (China Salesman, Wolf Warrior 2, My War,...).

He fluently speaks Kirundi, French, English and Mandarin Chinese. Moreover, he makes light conversations in Swahili, Arabic and Dutch. Chris is fond of reading, photography (black-and-white formats), outdoor sports activities (cycling, swimming) and coding (Javascript/Python).
